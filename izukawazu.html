Izu Kawazu (Japan)

Kawazu, Japan

Kawazu, on the east coast of the Izu Peninsula, is famous for its early-blooming Kawazu Sakura (mid-February to early March), painting the town pink while much of Japan is still cold. Part of Fuji-Hakone-Izu National Park and a UNESCO Global Geopark, Kawazu also offers hot springs, fresh seafood, and locally grown wasabi.

Highlights:

Kawazu Cherry Blossom Road: 4 km along the Kawazu River.

Kawazu Seven Waterfalls (Nanadaru): Easy hikes and outdoor hot spring near Odaru Falls.

Imaihama Beach: Summer swimming, sunbathing, and fireworks in August.

Other attractions: IZOO sensory zoo, Kawazu Bagatelle Park (rose garden & café), Funado no Banya seaside onsen, and the Kawazu Nanadaru Loop Bridge.

Activities & Cuisine:

Year-round hot springs, summer canyoning and paragliding, autumn hiking in the Amagi Mountains, fresh seafood with wasabi, and stays in traditional ryokans. Nearby towns include Higashi Izu and Shimoda.
