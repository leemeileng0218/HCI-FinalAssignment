Cheng Hoon Teng Temple Melaka (Malaysia)

Cheng Hoon Teng (also known as the "Clear Cloud Temple") is Malaysia's oldest functioning Chinese temple, located in the heart of Malacca's UNESCO World Heritage Site core zone.

Built in 1645 during the Ming Dynasty era, it was constructed by local Chinese merchants and dedicated to the Goddess of Mercy (Guanyin). The temple showcases a blend of Chinese, Malay, and European architectural styles, reflecting Malacca's multicultural heritage.

Key Features

  • Intricate wood carvings, ceramic figurines, and ornate roof decorations
  • A main hall housing a sacred statue of Guanyin
  • Side halls dedicated to other deities including the God of Prosperity and God of War
  • Traditional courtyard with a lotus pond
  • Original stone wells and antique artifacts dating back centuries

Visiting Information

Location: Jalan Tokong, 75200 Malacca City, Malacca

Opening Hours: Daily 7:00 AM – 7:00 PM

Admission: Free (donations welcome)

Note: Modest attire is required – shoulders and knees must be covered. Remove shoes before entering prayer halls.

Cultural Significance

The temple is a symbol of Malacca's long-standing multicultural harmony. It has been designated as a National Heritage Site and remains an important place of worship for local Chinese communities. During festivals like Chinese New Year and the Guanyin Birthday Celebration, the temple draws large crowds of devotees and tourists alike.
