Miniature Kuala Lumpur (Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia)

Miniature Kuala Lumpur is a unique scale model park located in the heart of Malaysia’s capital city, offering a fascinating miniature journey through Kuala Lumpur’s most iconic landmarks and architectural marvels. Built at a 1:25 scale, the park brings the city’s skyline and historical sites to life in intricate detail, making it an educational and entertaining attraction for visitors of all ages.

The park features over 50 meticulously crafted models, including the Petronas Twin Towers, KL Tower, Merdeka Square, Sultan Abdul Samad Building, National Mosque, and Istana Negara. Each model is built with precision, incorporating tiny details like miniature vehicles, people, and even working lights that illuminate the displays after dark. Interactive information panels accompany each exhibit, sharing interesting facts about the landmarks’ history, design, and cultural significance.

Key highlights include a 360° panoramic view of the miniature cityscape, a dedicated children’s play area with mini construction toys, and a 4D cinema that showcases Kuala Lumpur’s transformation over the decades. The park also houses a gift shop selling miniature replicas of local landmarks and a cafe serving Malaysian snacks and beverages.

Getting There and Around

The park is conveniently located within walking distance of popular Kuala Lumpur attractions like Merdeka Square and the Sultan Abdul Samad Building. From KL Sentral Station, take the LRT Kelana Jaya Line to Masjid Jamek Station (10 minutes), then walk for 5–8 minutes to reach the park. Alternatively, take a taxi, ride-hailing service, or city tour bus directly to the entrance—limited paid parking is available nearby.

The entire park is wheelchair and stroller accessible, with ramps and wide pathways connecting all display areas. Guided tours are available in English, Malay, Mandarin, and Japanese at scheduled times throughout the day, providing in-depth insights into the landmarks on show.

Visit during weekday mornings to avoid crowds, or in the evening to see the illuminated models. Book tickets online in advance for discounted rates, and consider purchasing a combo ticket that includes entry to nearby attractions like the National Textile Museum. Don’t miss the interactive "Build Your Own KL" workshop for kids, and pick up a miniature souvenir from the gift shop to remember your visit.
