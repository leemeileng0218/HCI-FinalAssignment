Mount Qing Cheng (China)

Mount Qingcheng, located in Dujiangyan City of Sichuan Province, is one of the birthplaces of Taoism in China and a UNESCO World Cultural Heritage Site (inscribed together with the Dujiangyan Irrigation System in 2000). Known as "the Most Serene under Heaven", it is celebrated for its lush evergreen forests and numerous Taoist temples. Laojunge (Laojun Pavilion), situated atop Pengzu Peak (the highest peak of the front mountain at an elevation of 1,600 meters), is the iconic landmark of the mountain, housing a statue of Laozi riding a green ox.

The mountain is divided into the Front Mountain and Rear Mountain. The Front Mountain (15 square kilometers) is the core cultural area, featuring over 30 Taoist sites like Jianfu Palace (built in the Tang Dynasty), Tianshi Cave (where Zhang Daoling, founder of Taoism, practiced), and Shangqing Palace. The Rear Mountain (100 square kilometers) offers pristine natural scenery with valleys, waterfalls, and ancient villages such as Tai'an Ancient Town and Youyi Village.

Laojunge stands at the summit, providing panoramic views of Dujiangyan City and the surrounding landscapes on clear days. Inside the pavilion, cultural relics and Taoist scriptures are displayed, showcasing the profound influence of Taoism on Chinese culture. The mountain also preserves traditional Taoist music, medicine, and architecture, making it a vital hub for Taoist heritage.
