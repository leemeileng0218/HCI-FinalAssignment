Okayama (Japan)

Okayama is the capital of Okayama Prefecture, located in western Japan's Chugoku region. Nicknamed the "Land of Sunshine" for its relatively low rainfall, it’s known for iconic historical sites, delicious cuisine, and as a gateway to nearby attractions like Miyajima Island.

Top Attractions

Okayama Korakuen Garden

One of Japan's "Three Great Gardens," built in the early 1700s. Features spacious lawns, ponds, tea houses, and views of Okayama Castle.

Okayama Castle (Crow Castle)

A reconstructed feudal castle with black exterior walls (earning it the "Crow Castle" nickname). Houses a museum showcasing samurai history and artifacts.

Kurashiki Bikan Historical Quarter

A preserved Edo-period district with white-walled warehouses, canals, and willow trees. Home to art museums, craft shops, and cafes.

Momotaro Shrine

Dedicated to Momotaro (the "Peach Boy" of Japanese folklore), a beloved local hero. The shrine hosts annual festivals and has beautiful grounds.

Practical Information

  • Location: Chugoku Region, Okayama Prefecture, Japan
  • How to Get There:
    • Shinkansen: ~2.5 hours from Tokyo, ~1 hour from Osaka to Okayama Station
    • Air: Okayama Airport has flights to Tokyo, Sapporo, and international routes to Seoul
  • Local Cuisine: Try Okayama-style ramen, kurashiki sweets, and "kibi dango" (Momotaro's favorite snack)
  • Best Time to Visit: Spring (cherry blossoms) and autumn (fall foliage)
  • Official Tourism Site: Okayama Tourism Official Website

Nearby Highlights

Take a short trip from Okayama to visit:

  • Miyajima Island (with its floating torii gate)
  • Takamatsu and the Seto Inland Sea islands
  • Hiroshima Peace Memorial Park
