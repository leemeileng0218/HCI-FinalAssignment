Cui Lake Kunming (China)

The lake is surrounded by weeping willow trees, manicured gardens, and historic pavilions—including the iconic Daguan Pavilion, which dates back to the Qing Dynasty. Several small islands dot the lake’s surface, connected by elegant stone bridges like the Double Dragon Bridge and the Jade Bridge. The lake’s shores are lined with teahouses, kiosks selling local snacks, and open spaces where residents gather to practice tai chi, fly kites, or perform traditional music.

Key highlights include the Yunnan University campus adjacent to the lake (with its colonial-era buildings), the Cuihu Park Zoo (home to rare Yunnan golden monkeys), and seasonal events like the Spring Festival lantern displays and summer lotus flower exhibitions. Boat rides are a popular way to explore the lake, with options for rowboats, pedal boats, and guided tours on traditional wooden vessels.

Weather Information

Current Conditions (as of Jan 17, 2026): Sunny with temperatures ranging from 8°C to 20°C (46°F to 68°F). RealFeel® temperature matches actual readings due to low humidity (35–45%). Gentle wind speed of 5–10 km/h from the west, with clear skies and excellent visibility. Air quality index is 42 (good).

Year-round Climate: Mild and consistent—Kunming is known as the "Spring City" with temperatures ranging from 10°C to 25°C. Winter (Dec–Feb): Cool and dry with occasional light frost in the early morning—ideal for walking around the lake. Summer (Jun–Aug): Warm and slightly rainy with temperatures rarely exceeding 30°C—perfect for boat rides and enjoying lotus blooms.

Recommended Gear: Layered clothing (mornings and evenings can be cool, afternoons warm), comfortable walking shoes, sunscreen (UV rays are strong at high elevation), sunglasses, and a light raincoat or umbrella for occasional summer showers.

For up-to-date forecasts, check AccuWeather or weather.com.

Getting There and Around

The lake is located in central Kunming, easily accessible by public transport. Take metro Line 2 to Cuihu Park Station (5-minute walk to the lake entrance), or use bus routes 2, 5, 10, or 23 to reach various stops around the lake. Taxis and ride-hailing services are also widely available throughout the city, with drop-off points at all major park entrances.

The lake and surrounding park are entirely walkable, with paved paths circling the water (approximately 3.5 km total). Wheelchairs and strollers can access most areas, with ramps at bridges and pavilion entrances. Public restrooms, drinking water stations, and seating areas are located regularly along the shore.

Visit early in the morning (6:00 AM–9:00 AM) to watch locals practice tai chi and enjoy the lake at its quietest, or in the late afternoon (4:00 PM–6:00 PM) for beautiful sunset views. Try local snacks like rice noodles, flower cakes, and roasted chestnuts from kiosks around the lake. After your visit, explore nearby attractions like the Yunnan Provincial Museum and the Green Lake Mosque.
