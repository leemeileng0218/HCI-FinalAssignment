Amanohashidate (Japan)

Amanohashidate: Japan’s Bridge to Heaven

Amanohashidate, located in Miyazu City, Kyoto Prefecture, is one of Japan’s famous “Three Scenic Views.” This 3.6-km sandbar, lined with nearly 5,000 black pine trees, is said to be a heavenly ladder that fell from the sky. When viewed upside down (mata nozoki), it looks like a bridge connecting heaven and earth.

Top spots include Kasamatsu Park and Amanohashidate View Land, both offering stunning panoramic views. Visitors can also explore historic sites like Chion-ji Temple and enjoy activities such as kayaking and boat rides.

Beautiful in every season and known for fresh seafood from Miyazu Bay, Amanohashidate is a must-visit destination for nature and culture lovers alike.
