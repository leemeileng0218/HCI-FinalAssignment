Du Jiang Yan (China)

Dujiangyan Irrigation System

The Dujiangyan Irrigation System, built over 2,000 years ago, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of China’s greatest engineering achievements. Located near Chengdu, it uniquely controls water flow without a dam, making it both functional and scenic.

Best time to visit: Spring and autumn offer mild weather and clear views. Visit early morning or late afternoon to avoid crowds and enjoy softer light for photos.

Getting there: Take a high-speed train from Chengdu to Dujiangyan (under 1 hour), then a short taxi or shuttle ride to the site.

What not to miss: Walk across Anlan Cable Bridge, visit Erwang Temple, and pair your trip with nearby Mount Qingcheng.

What to wear: Comfortable walking shoes and layers; bring a light rain jacket in summer. Food tip: Try local Sichuan dishes like sweet water noodles or mapo tofu at restaurants near the entrance.

A visit to Dujiangyan combines history, nature, and culture—perfect for a rewarding day trip from Chengdu.
